# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Thai collectible enthusiasts ("Owners") who take their hobby seriously. They're building a personal record of what they have, what they're hunting, and what has passed through their hands. They use this app in short focused sessions: logging a new acquisition, updating a state, checking their totals. They know their collection intimately; the tool should get out of their way and trust that.

A smaller audience is the people who receive public Share Cards — anyone the Owner chooses to show a modong to. They see a single card with no prices or private notes; they don't have accounts.

## Product Purpose

Dongmodel is a personal collection ledger for Thai collectors. It stores every Modong (acquired piece), tracks Wanted Items (the hunt), and lets Owners share individual pieces or groups publicly without exposing prices or private notes.

Success looks like: an Owner opens the app, logs a new piece in under a minute, and can answer "what do I have, what am I looking for, and how much did this cost me" at any moment. The share link they send a friend shows the piece, not the price.

## Brand Personality

Collector culture, niche pride. The app belongs to the community of people who know what "กำลังงมเข็ม" means and find it funny. It's not a startup tool or a productivity suite. It's closer to a personal ledger kept by someone who knows their stuff — confident, direct, a bit underground. Not corporate. Not social.

Three words: grounded, collector-coded, unshowy.

## Anti-references

- **Social media feeds**: No algorithmic ordering, no engagement signals, no "who liked this." The collection is private by default; sharing is a deliberate act, not a default state.
- Anything that looks like Shopee, Etsy, or a marketplace: no price-forward layouts, no "buy now" affordances, no product-grid shopping UI.
- Generic SaaS dashboards: no blue-primary, no "streamline your workflow" voice, no enterprise chrome.

## Design Principles

1. **The collection speaks, the UI frames it.** Photos and names are the content. UI chrome should recede; never compete with the collector's own material.
2. **Private by default, shared by choice.** Prices, notes, and personal details live behind the Owner's session. Every share is intentional; the interface reflects that default privacy.
3. **Collector voice, not tech voice.** Thai collector community language is the natural register. The states, phrases, and names in the domain are the personality — preserve them literally.
4. **One record, one piece.** No quantity fields, no catalog logic, no marketplace thinking. This is a personal ledger; each modong is its own thing.
5. **Earn trust through consistency.** The same button, the same form control, the same card pattern across every screen. Reliability in a tool used for personal records is a form of respect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Thai primary, with some English UI chrome (Login, Register, Admin) — both must be readable together without visual conflict. WCAG AA as the floor: 4.5:1 body contrast, 3:1 large text. No specific reduced-motion requirements stated, but the collector audience spans ages; conservative motion defaults are appropriate.
